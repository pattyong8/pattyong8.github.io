HS Hangouts

  • With HS Friends
  • 2012-2015
  • The many beach days, GC memes, and HS parties

High school was a period of intense character development and comparing contrasting viewpoints. On one hand, I had my more conversative Newport Beach friends group, and on the other, I had a mix of friends that were more progressive. I enjoyed hanging out with both these friends groups and, similar to a relationship, it helped me determine what I wanted and did not want out of my college friend groups. Overall, I had many great memories shared with my high school friends that will always be cherished.

Some Memories...

1. My Newport Beach friends had been my friends since elementary school. Each individual on their own were great in their own way. As a group, they became more conservative and egged each other on. Because of our history, I did not let that bother me and continued being friends with them all. We had some great memories since a handful of the group also swam and played water polo with me in college. This was my default friend group. My best friend in this group was JR. He and I had similar situations with our fathers and were interested in mostly the same things. He helped me through my tough times in high school and was one of the first people I came out to. Super supportive and a great friend.

2. My other friend group mostly came from folks from Y&G. We all were outspoken, driven, and hungry students that wanted to push our limits and succeed in life. This became one of the must have characteristics that I would look for in any friend group in college. My best friend in this group was Alex. He and I both did ASB and Y&G together among other things. We both were smart, emotionally intelligent, and hard working. I continue, to this day, cherish our friendship and regularly stay in touch.

3. I took it upon myself to mentor a few of the underclassmen I came into contact with. One of these individuals was Davis. Davis and I worked on his public speaking, life aspirations, and social skills. It was moments like this that affirmed my passion for mentoring and teaching high school students. It is such a critical moment in their lives where folks can change the trajectory of their lives.
