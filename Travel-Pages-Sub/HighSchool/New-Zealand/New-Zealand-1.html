New Zealand 2012 & 2014

  • With CDM Water Polo Team
  • Summer of 2012 & 2014
  • International Water Polo Tournament

Our bi-yearly team water polo trip to compete in the New Zealand National tournament consists of many of my favorite memories in high school. Playing against such high-level competition and sharing amazing experiences with my teammates contributed to our sense of unity. I remember competing against 25+ year old as a 15-year-old freshman in 2012 and getting to the finals as a senior in 2014. There were many fun memories full of activities and food :).

Heading off

Picture #1
Picture #2

We all gathered at CDM to take a few team pictures before leaving for the airport. I was so unbelievably excited to go to New Zealand for the second time in high school. As a Varsity started, I would be tested and relied on to perform at my best every game. For context, my position was on the 4-5 side (right side facing the opponent’s goal). I viewed myself as a play maker and utility player. Outside of water polo, I was excited to build upon my existing friendships and grow as a person.

The city of Auckland was beautiful. It reminded me of Seattle since both of these locations had a large needle like structure. I got so many amazing photos of the city. Our accommodations were at a “Uni”, basically a high school in the US. I chose to stay in the individual rooms instead of the shared rooms with the other seniors and women’s team because most of my friends were placed in the individual rooms. When we first arrived at the school, we were greeted by a student group performing a traditional haka. It was so interesting to watch. Because our team was full of immature high schoolers, most of my peers laughed which was disrespectful but they couldn’t help it.

Water Polo Tournament

Picture #8
Picture #9

We played most of our games at the main stadium which I got a sick paramo of. This is also where the opening ceremony is always held. All of my friends on the team were placed on the JV team, while I was in Varsity as a starter. Because of this, I did not get to hang out with them for almost all of the trip. At first, I was pretty disappointed but then realized it was alright since it would be nice to concentrate on my self-improvement and water polo skills. As a Varsity team, we made it all the way to the finals before losing to the New Zealand national team. It was such a close game full of aggression, questionable referee calls, and stellar plays.

Our team has a tradition to always visit Black Sands Beach and dip into a nearby ice cold waterfall pool. Black Sands Beach is soooo beautiful. We always hike up to the top of a rock on the beach that has sweeping views of the entire beach. It is always so nice to get pictures from up here. After spending time here, our team would start on a hike that lead to an isolated waterfall in the middle of the surrounding forest. The water is always freezing but so refreshing. There were many great photos at this location.

This year, our team surprised us with a secret activity. We drove for around 2 hours before reaching our destination somewhere in a nearby mountain range. We were going to go mountain luging! We went on a few runs before the varsity team went on one run and specifically targeted our coach and tried to flip/crash into him haha.

It was fun being able to hang out with the other guys outside of the Varsity team. This meant I was able to see Vincent every now and there and hang out with the JV guys. We got to eat fishmonger! Our team literally goes crazy over this stuff. It ain’t cheap but it tastes soooo amazing. :) Overall, this trip was an amazing memory, exposed me to international travel, and built closer ties to my team and CDM friends. This was an interesting phase in my life where I was trying to figure out who I was and how to become more authentically myself. This trip helped me understand who my friends were and greatly improved my water polo mental and physical abilities.
