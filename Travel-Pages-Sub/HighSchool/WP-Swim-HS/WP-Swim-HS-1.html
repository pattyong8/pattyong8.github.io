HS Water Polo & Swimming

  • With CDM Team
  • 2012-2015
  • Water Polo Games, Swim Meets, & Practice

Water polo and swimming was the foundation of my high school career. Not only did it take almost every waking moment of my freetime, but I threw myself fully into the process and succeeded because of it. Our team played at the division 1 level, got to CIF semi-finals, and traveled internationally to play in New Zealand and Australia. This 14 year experience taught me grit, teamwork, and determination. The grueling morning practice, 5 hours of practice + weights, and competitive games all contributed my hard worker mentality.

Some Memories...

1. The part of practice dreaded most by our team were the infamous 10x200 we did day after day after day. It was our coaches way of making sure we were in shape and could outswim any team out there. While the rest of the team hated this set, I personally didn’t mind it since I actually did love swimming as well lol.

2. During my senior year, our coach recruited a few of his retired Navy Seal friends to run a beach day practice with our Varsity team. OMG this was by far one of the hardest practices I had ever gone through but I just put my head down and powered through the day. What an experience.

3. During my junior to senior summer, my team competed in JOs as a high school team. This was actually kinda rare since most teams were “super teams” composed of the best players from multiple different teams. Even with this obvious advantage, we went on to win 1st place in the gold division further solidifying our abilities as a team.

4. Swimming was another passion of mine. I stopped swimming for the Azot club team in middle school but never lost the love and technical skill I had learned. Because of this, I went on to make the varsity team my freshman year at the division 1 level. We actually won CIF my Sophomore year! My best strokes were backstroke and freestyle. I started off as a backstroker freshman year, to being a distance 500 yard swimmer my sophomore year, to being a sprinter my junior and senior year.

5. My brother and I received many awards during our time with the CDM aquatics high school program. We got the coaches award multiple years in a row! It was mostly a result of our hard work ethic and extremely coachability.
