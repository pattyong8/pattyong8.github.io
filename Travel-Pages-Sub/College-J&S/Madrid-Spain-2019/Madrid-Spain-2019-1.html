Madrid/Barcelona 2019

  • With Family
  • Mar 21st-Apr 1st 2019
  • Visiting Vincent During His Study Abroad Semester

Just as my family came and visited me during my study abroad adventure in China, we went to go visit Vincent during his time in Spain. We got to explore all the amazing places near his residence. We went clubbing, tapas hunting, sightseeing, and even ziplining. There were so many amazing meals and we got to splurge since the dollar went longer in Spain. This was a trip for the books!

Day 1

Picture #1
Picture #2

Spring Break just started and I found myself on a plane heading to visit Vincent in Spain! He decided to spend his study abroad semester in Madrid, Spain. What a wonderful choice. It also gave our family the opportunity to vacation in Europe. I had a layover in the UK before transferring to Spain. I remember, it was quite the situation having 4-5 hours to kill in the UK airport.

I landed in Spain and found my way to Vincent’s place! We cooked and drank at his place in preparation for the night to come. He apparently got a crew together to go out the night I arrived. We had a simple meal of chicken breast and vegetables on top of rice [Picture #1]. Once we finished our meal, we played a mini game of snappa before his friends came.

The crew arrived and we hosted a rager pregame before heading off to a club called El Kapital [Picture #2]. OMG it was a 7 story club! Each floor had a different theme and created a multi-tier setup that anyone could enjoy. We picked a spot on the 5th floor to host everyone we came with and a few others that showed up after. We got a few drinks and started partying the night away.

At some point we found ourselves on the top floor of the club where food and drinks were being served. It was apparently the smoking floor as well since everyone was smoking a cigarette. I found myself talking to a cute guy that was so my type. Turns out he was gay and we ended up hooking up for a bit haha. The night came to an end and Vinc and I went back to his place so that we could pick up mom the next day.

Day 3 of Madrid was full of food, sight, and cultural exploration. We started the afternoon at a nearby market called Plaza de Cascorro [Pictures #15 & #16]. It was a full on tourist trap. There were tons of tents tightly packed onto one street, each selling their own version of tourist souvenirs. I found a funny shirt that referenced a ton of F.R.I.E.N.D.S quotes and sent it to Connor and Matt since they are fans of the show [Picture #17]. Although catered to tourists, it felt somewhat authentic to Spain's culture.

Mom and I had been eyeing a restaurant called Sumo Sushi [Picture #26]. It claimed to be an all you can eat sushi restaurant, however, we learned that it was actually a 3 course meal. Observation: From what I have gathered so far Spain’s culture is about eating small servings of many things but not over eating. Thus this “buffet” place was as much of a buffet a place in Spain can get. Vincent had school on this day so our plan was to explore El Retrio Park and eat Sumo Sushi without him and meet him at the Royal Palace in the afternoon. Mom and I didn’t have phone service so we set a time with Vincent and fully crossed our fingers that we would meet up at that time.

Okay so TBH, I mixed up a few of the events on the first day so I will just note here that on a separate day, we did the whole “meet Vincent at the royal palace thing”.

After we had enough of the palace, we continued to walk around the city until dinner time. Vincent then took us to multiple places to snack. Day 5

Picture #39

This was our day trip to Toledo! It was around an hour away from Madrid and Vincent had been waiting to visit this city until we came to visit him. We got up around 6am to catch the earliest bus. Mom made sure that we grabbed pastries from a store around the block before we left lol. The bus was located at a bus terminal that we took transportation to get to.

Picture #40
Picture #41
Picture #42

I tried to sleep during the bus ride but it honestly wasn’t the most comfortable situation so I mostly just snoozed. Eventually, I woke up to the bright sun and started to look around. OMG I could see the city of Toledo in the distance! Wooohoo! I got a great panoramic view of the surrounding city and landscape [Picture #39]. Before getting to the main part of the city, we walked around the surrounding street at the ground level just to get a feel for the more rural areas of Spain. The architecture was beautiful and the community was vibrant [Pictures #40 & 41].

We eventually made our way of the hill to the main part of the city. When we first arrived, we stopped at the visitor center to get a map to better understand where the heck we were in the city. Our first stop was a free museum full of art pieces from the city [Pictures #42 & 43]. There were paintings, sculptures, and random artifacts. After this, we decided we wanted to explore deeper into the city so we went straight for one of the main attractions, the church.

The church was absolutely beautiful [Picture #44]! There were so many tourists around taking pictures, walking around, and just finding a cool place for shade. There were many school groups on either field trips or random spring break programs from around the world.

After visiting the church and walking around a bit more, we sought out lunch for the day. We found a tucked away restaurant without that many people in it. We got a few beers and ordered a dish each [Picture #45]. I remember the dishes being interesting. I think one of them was a duck dish, another was boar, etc. Either way, our meal was wonderful and we left with a very full belly.

Mom always has dessert on her mind so we stopped by a sweets shop that had a plethora of different candies. Mom got a few sweets to try since she was looking for something to bring back for her office to share. In this same shop was a Guinness World Record for the tallest marzipan sculpture [Pictures #46 & #47]!

We continued walking around the city. Mom took a nap on a bench while Vincent and I hung around a park waiting for her to wake up [Picture #48]. The surrounding area was gorgeous. A river wrapped around the city on one side. There were birds everywhere. Eventually mom woke up so we decided to keep walking around the far side of the city until we happened upon a zipline tourist attraction.

I remember trying to convince Vincent and mom that the zipline was a waste of money and would be so not worth it but Vincent had another perspective. He said that since the cost was only $15 per person, it was so worth having the memory of spontaneously ziplining in Toledo. I am glad we listened to him because it was a whole lot of fun. Each of us gave it a shot, I got a sticker for the experience to put on my water bottle, and it was honestly kinda a wild ride ziplining across a river [Pictures #49 - #51].

The city and surrounding area looked even better during golden hour. What a wonderful day trip and mini excursion for the books.
