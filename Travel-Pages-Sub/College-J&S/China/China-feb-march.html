China Study Abroad

  • With Connor, Jake, Antonia, Ryan, Kevin, Fisher, Seabass, Wyatt, Essi, Nanna, Francois, Timo, and Trine
  • Feb 26th-Jun 18th, 2018
  • College Study Abroad

This study abroad opportunity was one of the most defining moments in my college career. Not only did I get to explore more of myself and family culture, I also immensely improved my Mandarin and made some amazing new friends. It strangely enough felt like I was at home and fit right in. I think this was due to my childhood exposure to Chinese/Taiwanese culture. Most of the friends I made were from other countries outside of China and helped create a study abroad adventurous environment. Thank goodness Connor and I went together because it allowed me to try new things knowing that I always had a homie by my side. So glad I gutted up and went abroad. So many amazing memories and experiences to last a lifetime.

Arrival & Orientation

While on the plane to China, I experienced a number of different feelings: excitement, worry, and anxiety amongst others. This was going to be the first time in my life where I ventured for more than a month outside the safety of my home in California. I was traveling to a foriegn land without my family or much guidance. There were many unknowns but I could feel the excitement building. Thankfully, I was studying abroad with my good friend Connor. Having him there provided the bit of confidence I knew I would need to blossom as a person, student, and growing adult.

Landing and figuring out how to get to my new apartment was interesting. My Mandarin was okay since I grew up listening to my parents speak to each other. I started talking with the Taxi driver and asked him about all the good food around, places to visit, and weather; all things I knew how to say. I got to my apartment and figured out the room situation. Connor and I decided to double, which was a great call, so we could pay lower rent. Because I got there before Connor I got to choose my bed first, though it didn’t really matter all that much. I had the place all to myself and got settled in.

The next morning, I woke up and went to explore the surrounding area. I walked to a mall called Wujiaochang. It was a massive collection of around four buildings. It was actually here that I got my first meal; a shaobing made in a hot oven [Pictures #1 & #2]. Soooo yummy! I also went to go figure out my phone card and bank account since this was a priority being in a different country. I had to call my mom and ask her to translate for the more complicated stuff lol.

Connor arrived that night! Fischer also arrived sometime during the day. It was so great to have a familiar face there and another Cal student. Fischer had already been to China once so he decided to take us to the Bund that night for a celebratory drink. We ended up going to a rooftop bar and got a few beers [Picture #3]. After the beers, we went back and got ready for the next official study abroad orientation day.

The orientation went well! I found myself being more extroverted during the session. I forced myself to meet a ton of new people and ended up making friends quite quickly. One of the first people I met was a guy named Wyatt. He was from Australia and we ended up meeting a number of other australians as well such as Jake and Antonia. Our other friends included Magnus, Ryan, Kevin, Sebas, Essi 1, Essi 2, Trine, Nanna, Francois, Jaako, Kristina, and Timo [Picture #4]. The Fudan organizers planned a dumpling making session for us which was super fun! Since I had experience making them, I was kinda pumping them out haha [Pictures #5 & #6].

Picture #7

We actually hit it off so well that I started coordinating a night out with everyone we met. We ended up inviting everyone back to our apartment and had a pregame before heading out to propaganda for the night. Omg this was such a fun night! We got smashed with everyone and made a ton of new friends very quickly [Picture #7]. Propaganda was great as well. Since we were Americans, we got free drinks lol. They also let us in for free. The interesting part was that anyone who looked asian, like myself, needed to be with other white friends in order to get the wristband denoting you as the free entry/free drink customer. This was also the night that I met Kevin. Did some interesting things that night haha.
