China Study Abroad

  • With Connor, Jake, Antonia, Ryan, Kevin, Fisher, Seabass, Wyatt, Essi, Nanna, Francois, Timo, and Trine
  • Feb 26th-Jun 18th, 2018
  • College Study Abroad

This study abroad opportunity was one of the most defining moment in my college career. Not only did I get to explore more of myself and family culture, I also immensely improved my Mandarin and made some amazing new friends. It strangely enough felt like I was at home and fit right in. I think this was due to my childhood exposure to Chinese/Taiwanese culture. Most of the friends I made were from other countries outside of China and helped create a study abroad adventurous environment. Thank goodness Connor and I went together because it allowed me to try new things knowing that I always had a homie by my side. So glad I gutted up and went abroad. So many amazing memories and experience to last a lifetime.

After the speak easy we walked around for a bit before hitting up a random backpackers bar. It was very empty and didn’t have a good vibe. Also, the Mexican food looked to be questionable. At this location we met a father who was going out while his son was staying in eating pizza and chilling. There was also a lady that came in after us, talked to the waiter for 10 seconds and then put her head on the bar and proceeded to fall asleep. After this we got food at a random udon and bbq place. Noah thought it was something that it was not so he only got a beer. I got udon with a good soft boiled egg. After this, we went back to the hotel and met Ricardo and Nina. Two super cool people Ricardo was from Norway and Nina was from Germany. [Picture #3]They both met each other in Tokyo and decided to travel with each other to Osaka. We went for revolving sushi which was amazing for its price! Only 1 dollar a plate! Also, there was a self serve beer machine. Omg it was a great time and we went with our two new friends. There was also a game that would be played for every 5 plates we ate. It was a great way to get us to spend haha. I ate sooooo much sushi!

Afterwards we went to a billiards bar near dontonburi. Super low key but nice vibe. Met two more friends named Zane and ___ they were both from the UK and we’re good childhood friends. They decided to travel together and were having a good time. I talk to Zane for awhile. Talked about US politics, league, and why UK people say “that’s alright”. While we were playing pool and everyone was watching. I hit 3 shots in a row, two of which were very impressive. I think everyone was impressed. Couldn’t really hit a shot after that haha. Then I went to play darts for a bit with Noah and Ricardo. Dam we smelt like cigarettes after leaving the bar. I didn’t snake but being in a smoke room was not good for my clothes or lungs. Stopped at a family mart. Noah was looking for the sweet savory rice balls on a stick. After we left we went back to the hostel and fell asleep.
