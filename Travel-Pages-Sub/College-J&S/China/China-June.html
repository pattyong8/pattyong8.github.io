Classes at Fudan were not at the same caliber as UC Berkeley. Because of this, all our classes were super easy, thus, Connor and I were able to crush the classes we did take. A Series of Last Hurrahs

Picture #147
Picture #148
Picture #149

As June rolled around the corner, some of our friends including ourselves started to leave China to return home. Because of this, there were a series of “last hurrahs” and nights out to celebrate our amazing time together! The first of these hurrahs was one of my own creation. One of the things that I loved the most about China was the food. I grew up on this cuisine and it has a special place in my heart/stomach lol. Because of this, my personal last hurrah was a nice meal to one of the nicest restaurants serving traditional Shanghainese food. Seabass came with me and we enjoyed a wonderful meal out! I got braised pork belly, fired pigeon among many other amazing dishes [Pictures #147 - #149].

Our group last hurrah took place at one of our favorite bars to go to, La Bamba. It was an upbeat mexican themed restaurant that served decent American food, cheap drinks, and played amazing music. We all go together and celebrate our last night with everyone still in China. It was a huge deal as all our friends we made on the trip showed up to wish each other goodbye and we made sure to have fun while doing it [Pictures #150 & #151]! When it came to our travel squad, most of us were in tears over leaving each other. We all really had a special relationship with each other and it was sad to leave it behind. The La Bamba staff saw us all crying and tried to cheer us up by bringing us all La Bamba shirts [Pictures #152 - #154]. How sweet! I will truly miss this group and all our amazing memories: the ecafe session, myriad of trips around China, crazy nights out, and even gym sessions. Our entire group made sure to get a group photo before leaving [Picture #155].

Connor went home before I did. Because we had separate leave dates, folks also threw a goodbye night out celebration just for me! We started with a pre game on one of the Fudan student housing apartment roofs. We were def not supposed to be up there but it provided for a hell of a memory and view of the city [Picture #157]. Then we made our way to downtown Shanghai to go bar hopping. We even stopped at a random Irish place to watch a sporting event and drink some beers. It was overall a more chill goodbye night and a great way to get sent off [Picture #156, #158, & #159].

This study abroad opportunity was the experience of a lifetime. Not only did I get to discover who I was as a person, but I also got to meet many new friends, create lasting memories, and explore a new part of the world I have never been a part of. Like I mentioned previously, this trip allowed me to connect with a part of my culture that I never had enough time to deep dive before. I felt integrated, normalized, and at home in a weird way. The food was amazing, the vibes were on point, and I can’t wait to see how this shapes my perspective on life for years to come. REMEMBER, convince everyone you can to study abroad! It is 100% the RIGHT decision.
