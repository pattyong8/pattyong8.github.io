O4U Conferences 2018

  • With O4U Business and Marketing Friends
  • Sep 14th-23rd 2018
  • LGBTQ+ Leadership Conference

This was my second year attending the O4U conference. Honestly, it was such a blast and a great opportunity to meet a group of other driven LGBTQ+ individuals. First, I attended that O4U Marketing Conference hosted by Ogilvy. The second conference, O4U Business, was hosted by Goldman Sachs. This was a great time to catch up with old friends from the last conference and develop new connections.

O4U Marketing was just as fun as it was in 2017! I got to hangout with some of my old friends and made a ton of new friends along the way. I even got to reunite with Ryan from my study abroad program in China! I made him apply for O4U and he got it! Woooohoooo! :)

This year, O4U was in New York instead of Texas and boy, it was a complete 180 experience, for the better. New York just has a totally different and most adventurous vibe than the HQ of Pepsico lol. We got to stay in such a trendy hotel in the middle of NY. The rooms were so retro and perfectly fit the vibe of the marketing conference. We even got free drinks when we first got there!

Some of my previous friends included David, Julia, Taj, and Emily. New friends included Kyle, Hayden, and Winnie amongst others. The happy hours were well thought out, Oglivys HQ was trendy afff, and the company was pure gold! :)

O4U Business was a whole other beast. The folks that attended this conference were wayyyyyyy more competitive. It felt more like a weekend recruiting event as opposed to a conference to network and learn about a career. Because of this, the sponsor pool seemed but deeper and the events were more lavish.

It was much harder to make friends during this conference since many folks had already formed groups with other individuals that had been coming to this conference for years. I made some inroads with folks such as Turner and Tyler but everyone else seemed like they had an agenda and I wasn’t very much a part of it.

Either way, the accommodations were super nice and the events were boujee afffff. The Goldman headquarters was so nice and the facilitators were very informative. The career fair was also more well thought out. Over all, both of these conferences were an amazing opportunity to expose myself to more LGBTQ+ networks and become more comfortable with myself in general.
