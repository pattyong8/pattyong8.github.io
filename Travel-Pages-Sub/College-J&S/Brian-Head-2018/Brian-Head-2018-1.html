Brian Head, UT

  • With Fam + Doug & Coleman
  • Dec 26th-29th 2018
  • First Family Ski Trip

This was my family’s first skiing trip! Whooooohooooo! We went with my mom’s boyfriend, Doug and his son, Coleman. We all had such an amazing time on the mountain. It was very different from my only other time skiing. Instead of it being snowy and sloshy, it was cold, snowy, and powdery. Although I absolutely sucked at skiing, it was good to finally get into the sport.

We packed everything up and got ready to head to Utah! OMG we were all so excited for our first skiing trip together. The drive was around 10+ hours which honestly felt really long but eventually we found our way to Brian Head! We went straight to our Airbnb which was amazing! It was a one story home with multiple bedrooms, a cozy living area, and a backyard full of snow. We brought everything into the house and went to sleep early in preparation for the big ski day to come.

We woke up early the next morning, put on all our ski gear, and headed for the mountain. We had to drive there early to figure out the lift pass and ski rentals. Once we were on the mountain with our skis, we attempted a few green routes and spent most of the day learning how to ski. Honestly, we all sucked but it was loads of fun haha. We got a few quality family pictures along the way.

Honestly, the snow day was full of snowball fights, tubing, and snow angels. Our backyard was the perfect place to throw snowballs around and make snow angels. I got plenty of amazing photos of this. At one point, Vincent and Coleman were having a snowball fight. I got an amazing shot of Coleman throwing a snowball at Vincent.

We also went tubing at the nearby elementary school hill. Seemed like this spot was a local spot as many folks were around. We slid down the hill multiple times and got a real kick out of knocking each other over. We also had a football we were throwing around.

Couple of fun memories: We took a picture with mom and threw snow into her face as Doug was taking the photo. It resulted in an extremely funny reaction and photo to go with it. Another memory is when I went to the baseball field and spelt SPV in a heart with my foot in the snow. I made it huge since there was a ton of untouched snow.

On the way back from Brian Head, we stopped at Zion national park for a short exploration trip. We ended up just driving through the park and doing a short hike. We saw a whole family of deer, got amazing photos with the lighting and landscape, and crossed a sketchy bridge on the hike! It was a great pit stop and so worth the detour.

Overall, this trip was a major success and great way to bond with the entire family crew. Doug and Coleman are wonderful additions to the family. Can’t wait for the many other adventures we will go in!
