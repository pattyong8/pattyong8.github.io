Washington DC 2021

  • With Vincent, Erin, & Co.
  • June 22nd-29th 2021
  • Visiting Vincent

After receiving the offer from Eastdil Secured, I knew I needed to go visit Vincent before I switch careers. I am so glad I did. Vincent and I had a blast adventuring through DC, eating great meals, and lifting together. We saw all the famous monuments and even went to some local spots. I am so glad I got to spend time with my brother. I have missed having him around or in such close proximity. We get along so well and I am always happy when I get to hangout with him. To move adventures in the future!

Pictures

Vincent and I went to the gym almost every day I was there. This was because I was on a grind to get cut for my summer vacations. When I first arrived, the weather was so nice that we sat outside for most of the day. Working outside was actually so nice. Towards the end of the week, we had to move inside since it started to get much more humid. Vincent said the humidity was gone when I first arrived because it had rained the day before. One of the days we walked past the White House. There was a conspiracy theorist where all the tourists gathered to take photos. Very funny stuff. Vincent and I hoped in a hotel to freshen up since it was hot.

The day I arrived, we walked Embassy Row. It was fun guessing which flag represented what country. I had an instinctive knack for guessing the right country haha. We explored Georgetown University and happened to see Jon Ossoff in the aftermath of a speech to students. Students were bombarding him with photos from left and right. Vincent, Erin, and I went to explore Barcelona, a Spanish tapas restaurant. It was wonderful being able to catch up with Erin and Vincent. Vincent introduced me to his typical meal prep meal. It was actually very tasty and I was not surprised that he put something so tasty together.

I showed Vincent how to use landscape mode. It was applicable since we were taking photos in the architecture museum. We had a plan to visit the monuments on Sunday but the weather forecast said it was going to be the hottest day out of all the days I was visiting. Instead, we opted to explore them later in the day on Saturday since we were already out near the monuments. Georgetown was an amazing campus. When we first got there we were really confused because we were near the not so great looking student dorms. Once we ventured in a bit more, we understood why people love the campus.

Turns out Vincent loves ube pancakes just as much as I do lololol. I made sure to grab him three packs since they have a long shelf life. Their house bathtub was huge! Too bad it leaked into the room below it. A couple of days during the week we lifted at Erin’s and the other half were at Vincent’s gym. Funny memory was when I couldn’t do legs because my knee hurt. I was planning on Pelotoning alone but then Vincent decided to join me! That was so nice of him and it was so much fun. I will say that I kicked his ass in Peloton since I was more in cardio shape haha.

I made Vincent my famous blacken salmon with brussel sprouts and Quinoa. Vincent really wanted to try this ramen place nearby so we went. It was called Jinya. It was funny because we showed up in gym clothes. Their fried chicken was amazing but their ramen broth was okay. It had way too much ginger for my liking. Vincent got a care package from KPMG with a cool bucket list book and a few other goodies. The food from Barcelona was good but nothing crazy in my opinion. Great place to go out to dinner with people and try something new!

When we went to visit the White House, there was a full on protest going on for Black Votes Matter. We stood around and listened to the speakers for a bit before continuing on our exploration of the Mall. We passed by HUD! It was funny because I was studying for my real estate test so it was so relevant. Vincent and I used our Doordash at a sandwich show near the water. The sandwiches were actually amazing. Vincent said his was great in the moment but left him feeling very sluggish and heavy. We got to explore all the major buildings aka the Capitol, Supreme Court, and White House.

We toured using bikes which allowed us to see so many sights. We even made it to the Jefferson Memorial which is totally out of the way and not worth it if the tour was done walking. We went to a sushi restaurant one of the nights. It was really good for DC sushi. We went to Equinox on Sunday because it was so hot and we just wanted to relax. It was an amazing decision. We went to a food place called Farmers Fishers Bakers and it was divine. I had their ship Gnocchi and it was out of this world. First time having fresh Gnocchi. Was wonderful location for Vincent and I to hangout. We had a heart to heart about one of my crushes and relationships in general.

Overall, this trip was fantastic… Outside of the bug bites lol jk. I had such a great time and visiting Vincent was so refreshing. Hanging out with him is so easy because we are so close from all the years of cohabiting together. We did so many amazing things also. Enough of the touristy things but nothing too crazy. It was also so nice seeing Erin and catching up with her. Vincent seems to be doing well and I can’t wait to see how he grows. Until the next adventure with my unofficial twin.
