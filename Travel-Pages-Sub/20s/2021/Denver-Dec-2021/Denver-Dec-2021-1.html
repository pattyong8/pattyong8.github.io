Denver, CO (RE Trip)

  • With Quinn & Vincent
  • Dec 11th-Dec 12th 2021
  • Buying first property

Sometime in late 2021, Quinn and I started talking about purchasing our first investment property together in Denver. We realized this would be the perfect entry point into a great market with super low interest rates. Vincent was brought in as the third member and we all embarked on our investment property journey. After solidifying our goals and where in Denver we wanted to purchase, we made a trip out there to explore the area, meet our real estate agent, and gather in person as a team. This trip built the base of our partnership.

Pictures

This trip was a whirlwind of activity. I actually ended up pulling an all-nighter the Friday before we left because I needed to complete a few things at work. I went straight to the airport with Quinn at 4am and we began our 48 hour business trip to Denver. The flight there was memorable because it was the first time in my life that I passed out for an entire flight. This is usually difficult for me to do since I am a light sleeper. An emotionally draining all nighter will do that to you haha.

Once we arrived, we decided to use our Chase Sapphire Reserve card to get a free meal at an airport restaurant. We had to walk all the way to the end of terminal B to get to the restaurant which was about a 15mins walk. The food was below average but it was free so I can't really complain. Once we finished eating, we went to go find Matt’s car. He happened to be going out of town and was kind enough to let us borrow his car. We found his keys and took off towards Denver. I was exhausted from my all-nighter so I let Quinn drive and slept in the back as Vincent navigated.

We met Bryan, our real estate agent, and proceeded to check out 7 different houses in a variety of locations on the left half of Denver. Bryan was nice enough to drive us around and show us around. It was really nice because we were able to ask him questions and learn more about the Denver market. There were some houses with very interesting configurations. We only liked 2 out of the 7 houses we toured and noted them down. We realized that having a place that was as close to turn and key as possible was important to us. We thanked Bryan for his time and treated him to beers and finger food at Avanti.

After our dinner with Bryan, we checked into our hotel and I showed the guys around town. Since the hotel messed up our booking, Vincent was able to work some magic and get us two separate rooms! We each got our own bed. What a steal! I learned a lesson from Vincent that night haha. I started off the night by taking the guys to Frozen Matter for a speakeasy experience but the entire ice cream shop was packed with people. Because of this, we decided to forego the experience and go out in Rino. The guys had a blast! We met up with Evan and his girlfriend at Ratio. It was nice catching up with Evan and the guys were able to ask him questions about rent. We then hopped over to Bierstadt Lagerhaus where we stayed for the rest of our drinking shenanigans. We then hit up a pizza shop called Cart-Driver RiNo. They made amazing fresh pizza. After this we retired for the night.

The next day we woke up and packed up our things. We met at the hotel breakfast area and had some free breakfast. Nothing fancy but it did the job. We spent the next 4 hours working on models and considering purchasing a few of the properties we had toured. We had a very productive discussion and grew as a team that morning. Since Vincent had to leave relatively early we went to grab a bite before dropping him off at the airport. We ended up choosing a pho restaurant on the way to the airport. Vincent thought it was quite yummy.

After we dropped Vincent off at the airport, we decided to go visit Tavish, a friend of ours in the Denver area that had already bought a house and helped connect us with our real estate agent. We toured his house and saw how he was making it work on his end. Then we went out for ice cream at the dankest ice cream shop I ever been to. They incorporated marshmallows into their ice cream which gave it an unreal texture. We walked around for a bit, explored the neighborhood REI with the massive climbing wall, and caught up with Tav. We were nearing the end of our night and took Tav back to his house. So nice to see that guy. It’s always such a good time.

Quinn and I started heading to the airport to go get some free food from the airport restaurant again. Instead, we were met with some untimely traffic that had us stuck, not moving for an hour and a half. This was the worst traffic I had ever been in. To think if we had taken the toll road, we could have avoided all that… Bad luck haha. Quinn and I watch league videos while stuck in the car. Once we got to the airport we found out that the restaurant was closed which sucked. No worries though. We then headed to our gate and headed back home.

Overall this trip was a wild success. This was the first personal business trip I had done and was both very informative and tons of fun. Quinn and Vincent are amazing individuals to work with and I can’t wait for what the future holds for our group.
