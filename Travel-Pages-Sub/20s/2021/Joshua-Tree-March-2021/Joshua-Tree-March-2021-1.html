Joshua Tree March 2021

  • With Family
  • March 20th-21st 2021
  • Birthday Trip #1

For the second trip of my birthday weekend, we adventured out to Joshua tree for more climbing and hiking/scrambling! With Doug as our guide, we were able to explore some hidden caverns, hiking trails, and mountains of rock. Joshua Tree bouldering was hard but fun to try out! Lots of great photos and memories from a mecca for trad climbing.

Pictures

We made it to Joshua Tree! What a beautiful place. You would think a desert couldn’t be that beautiful but it truly was. Thankfully, it wasn’t blazing hot. Going in March was a smart decision on Doug’s end. Our first stop in the park was an area with a series of boulders called the hidden valley. We started at a boulder formation right in the parking lot called the Pothole Rock [Pictures #2 - #4]. There were a number of V0s and V Easys that honestly, were not that easy. This was the beginning of my understanding of the Joshua Tree Scale. I also tried a few other boulders including the triangle boulder, stem gem, and school boulder. All of them were insanely difficult. Nothing like gym climbing.

While at home I was able to meet up with JR and Davis [Pictures #17 & #18]. JR and I grabbed dinner just to catch up on life. We talked about careers, relationships, GC updates, etc. Davis and I were able to hangout during my birthday. He took me out to do whatever I wanted. Highlights include Hangar 24, poke bowls, and jacuzzing at a random hotel :). Vincent got me sushi randomly from Taiko for my birthday! It was an incredible selection and was soooo yummy [Picture #22]!

At school boulder, Vincent and I attempted this one short one move dyno climb which was super fun. Doug got a number of great aerial photos of us grabbing the edge. [Pictures #25 - #28]!

A funny memory was Vincent and I debating which Doritos chip flavor was superior between Nachos and cool ranch. Vincent argued for cool ranch and I advocated for Nacho. We devoured those Doritos that trip haha. [Picture #32]!

Doug took us through so many amazing parts of Joshua Tree. We explored caves, mountains, and even an oasis! The c avern was amazing [Pictures #29 - #31]! So much fun. We all wore helmets to stay safe. At one point towards the middle of the trek, we had to squeeze through a small chimney of rock. That was the highlight of the trek. Through our adventures, we got a number of great family photos [Pictures #33 - #36]. Overall, this was a successful trip!
