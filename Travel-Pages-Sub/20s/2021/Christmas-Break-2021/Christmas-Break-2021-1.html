Christmas Break

  • With Family
  • Dec 18th 2021-Jan 7th 2022
  • Irvine, CA

Christmas time is always such a wonderful time of year. I get to spend it with my family and relax while at home. Oh and don’t forget about all the yummy food! :) Being able to spend quality time with my mom and brother is a top priority for me. I also got to hangout with Davis almost everyday which was a huge plus. We saw Doug’s parents again for Christmas and went to a few restaurant celebration dinners. Yiyi was in town from Taiwan so we were able to see her often! Even being at Eastdil, I got 2 weeks at home.

Pictures

I got home on Dec 18th and was initially planning on going camping with Davis that night but there was a miscommunication. Davis also lost track of the days haha. Typical Davis :P. After hanging out with my family for a bit, I decided to go hangout with Davis anyway. We hangout at his place, hot tubbed, and play on his brand new Oculus. We also got Blaze Pizza. So yummy and I got a ton of toppings!

We were making a ton of Christmas cookies. I think Doug and mom went overboard on the Doug but it made sense since mom’s friends were supposed to come over to make cookies. I got mom and V a really cool necklace with Vincent’s tattoo engraved on a gold plated circle. They loved it and mom started to wear it all the time.

Team Fight Tactics was a hit with Vincent and Coleman. We had Coleman download it on his iPad and we ended up playing together. Davis joined us at some point. Doug made some amazing apple skivers.

Davis and I had our breakfast cook off! This was a long time coming and I was looking forward to kicking his butt. I was actually proven wrong and didn’t end up winning. Davis’s hash browns were actually on a whole level above frozen hash browns. I was pleasantly surprised with his hashbrowns and decided I would have to replicate them for my friends in SF.

Had dinner at Otoro Sushi and went all out to celebrate. The sushi here was amazing and hit the mark on almost all dishes. Personally, the salmon was the odd one out and did not taste as great as the rest of the fish. It was a bit more fatty and stringy. The otoro was also not my favorite but was still very fatty and tasty. We also got a round of A5 Japanese Wagyu sushi. Amazing!

Went to Mariners for Christmas and was wowed like I am every year. The vocals from the one performer always gives me the chills and the message from the preacher is always so wholesome. We made sure to go to the candle ceremony this year.

Alex and I got to hangout! We started our night at Hangar 24 with beers and food. Alex had never been there so I was glad I was able to show him such a wonderful spot. After, we hit up Diamond Jamboree for some Somi Somi. I got the milk and cream combo. It was fantastic.

Mastros with Yiyi was a treat! We got steaks, sides, wine, and even some appetizers. The shrimp appetizer was huge and so tender. The Ahi tower was fantastic. My steak was cooked perfectly but I had a hard time finishing it since I was so full on bread and the appetizers. However, I still had room for butter cake at the end!

Davis invited me over for dinner at his place for this birthday! His mom made an amazing garlic butter salmon, Oreo cookie ice cream cake, and perfectly cooked artichoke. I brought over a dessert of my own and we all celebrated Davis’s birthday together.

My last few days at home were filled with more sushi, a trip to Davis’s favorite Chinese restaurant and lots of quality time with my mom and my best friend Davis. The crispy noodle dish that Davis wanted me to try did not come out the way he expected which prevented us from trying his favorite dish from this place but not a worry. It just created an excuse to try the place again some time. I also treated Davis to his own sushi birthday dinner. We loved the first plate of sushi but the other orders were not my favorite. Overall, this break was amazing! So refreshing and I got some quality family and Davis time.
