Doug/Coleman Visit, Bee Sting, & Nate Moves

I got a very nice stainless steel pan from Goodwill! It was on a random day where I decided to go for a random walk. I found myself on a super trendy street called Haight Street. I popped in and out of a few stores and spent a bit of time at Goodwill where I found the pan. Doug and Coleman came and visited me in SF. We spent the day scootering around Golden Gate Park and exploring parts of the city. We got pizza for lunch but I opted out since I was cutting for Mexico. Later that night we went to 4505 BBQ. Doug paid for my meal which was very nice of him.

The big accident of this period was when I got stung in the head by a bee. I was on my way to Akbar and Daniel’s place when it happened. I decided to Revel there since it was cheaper and faster. I didn’t put down my helmet face shield which evidently could be used to block more than wind. A bee got stuck in my helmet and stung me. It took me a good 45 seconds to get the stinger out since I needed to pull over and take out my phone to see the sting. This resulted in one of the worst reactions I have ever had to any sting or bug bite. My entire face swelled up over the course of a few days to the pain where I couldn’t even open my eyes. My friends had to take me to urgent care where I got a steroid shot in the butt lol. That honestly hurt so much. I got work off though! XD

Sam and I got Udon in Japan town. Nate moved back to the Bay Area so we celebrated by going to a nice Korean BBQ restaurant called Umma. It was 100% overpriced but still very tasty. They were about to charge us double the price to cook the meat ourselves. I am so glad we were able to catch that before ordering table top. Davis convinced me to order a new mattress after I told him how I acquired my current mattress. I decided on the Dreamcloud Premier which honestly feels like a cloud.

Omakase w/ Jake

Jake and I decided to catch up over sushi sometime before I left for Seattle. I found a popular Omakase spot near Golden Gate Park that had raving reviews. To be honest, the restaurant wasn’t that packed so I am not sure what all the hype was about. The Omakase menu was a 8 course preselected array of nigri. Each had a special topping and came pre seasoned with soy sauce. Honestly, their soy sauce was very light and had no flavor. I had to add my own but that was fine. I ordered toro and A5 Wagyu beef. Jake loved it. This was a fantastic meal.
