I got to surprise mom and Vincent with their first big surprise Christmas gift ever! :) Read more for the details… New Year’s we spent with a small group but was a memorable occasion. I really got to bond more with Lucas, Sahar, and Connor. Cheers to more holiday breaks like this!

Christmas Surprise

Picture #14
Picture #13

Sooooooo. I wanted to surprise my mom and brother with new iPhones for Christmas since they still had their iPhone 6Ss and the iPhone 11 just came out! They rightfully deserved an upgrade! :) Jack was gracious enough to purchase the iPhones using his Apple employee discount. Brought the cost down by 15% which really only covered the tax + a little more LOL but helped bring the total cost down a ton. We schemed a plan to have me surprise my mom and brother by bringing them to the Fashion Island Apple store and surprising them by picking it up in store. I put in the order and couldn’t wait to get home and surprise them!

When the day came. I suggested we go to Fashion Island and take photos by the Christmas Tree [Picture #13]. It wasn’t hard since mom wanted to go anyways. We got our photos by the tree and enjoyed the day at Fashion Island. We walked around, stopped at Lulu, Peloton, and even the Tesla store before finally arriving at the Apple store. I had us all walk in and check out the phones.

Once we got far enough into the store, I told mom and Vincent to go up to an employee and ask where to pick up orders. OMG mom and Vincent were so confused and then slowly realized what I asked them to do. Neither of them wanted to go ask someone and kept on saying, stop it, what did you get?!? I decided to drag out the surprise a little more and just said that I was getting a case for my phone. They didn’t fully buy it but kinda calmed down. This was until I went up to the lady at the pick-up table and she confirmed my order for two iPhone 11s.

Mom and Vincent legit started freaking out and continued to do so even when the iPhones arrived at the table and when we left the store. They kept on insisting that we return the new iPhones and that the 6s was fine and not broken. Obviously, I was not going to accept that and just told them that I loved them both very much and they 100% deserved this. We had been through a lot as a family and they truly meant/mean so much to me.

Once we got past the, “return this now” phase. We got into the “OMG NO WAY” phase hahah. Vincent legit repeated that phrase 100 times as we walked through Fashion Island back to the car. He kept playfully punching me and laughing. Mom was honestly shocked and stayed that way until we got to Auntie Anne’s then she also started freaking out in her own way. :) I had them stand next to each other and got a picture of them smiling holding up their new iPhones [Picture #14]. The surprise was a success!!!

Cutover Situation

Picture #15

So, I still had to work during this break, and since my SM and team were so understanding, flexible, and amazing people, I got to work from home instead of having to be in the office for the first part of cutover. It allowed me to go home for a whole 2 weeks through Christmas! I had to go back on the 29th of December, and I had to work the night shift during our cutover. Honestly, it was way better than working normal hours since there was no traffic and I was able to enjoy the day before having to leave for work. Also, I was able to order dinner for myself :). Love those consulting perks. Also, tbh the workload was lighter at night.

Once we finished cutover, I was able to go back home again for another 2 weeks! WOW. I did not expect to get around a month off during winter break. 4 weeks total! Side note, I had an amazing boss and team which allowed me to spend all this time at home. This was such a privilege coming straight out of college. We all shared the same room while working together [Picture #15]. Also, there was a ton of free food during cutover which was just an added bonus. :)

New Year’s Celebration

Picture #16
Picture #17
Picture #18

I spent New Year’s Day and Eve working in the office at night, but it was alright since I still made it back in time to see the countdown and have a wonderful New Year’s Celebration with Lucas, Sahar, and Connor.

Basically, I finished work around 9pm and then Ubered straight from Viavi to Lucas and Sahar’s place in San Francisco. Connor met us there. We started the night by doing a short wine tasting [Picture #16]. Sahar gave me a very strong gin and tonic instead lol. Once we had our fair share of drinks and watched the New Year’s countdown, we went out to a nearby wine bar to hangout and talk for the rest of the night.

This place had only one other table occupied so we got the entire place, basically, to ourselves. Since Sahar and I were both the same person, we half hosted our own table. We kept grabbing some of the mini bites and bringing them to our table, pouring wine for Lucas and Connor, and chatted it up with owner/employees at this wine bar.

Sahar and Lucas had the wonderful idea of doing a self-reflection for New Years that consisted of something we learned about ourselves and something we wanted to work on for the New Year. I said that I wanted to work on being more emotionally connect with those around me. Sometimes I do come off as a bit overprepared and stiff. I wanted to loosen up and be naturally Pat. Some of this conversation started at the wine bar we were at but since Sahar and I were busy hosting and being our happy selves, we did not get a chance to dive deep into it.

Picture #19

The next morning, we took pictures by the Christmas Tree in Union Square and went for hotpot as our post New Year’s celebration meal [Pictures #17 & #18]. I was surprised that everyone was down for it but also not surprised that I was the most excited once we actually got there. I was hungover and Sahar convinced me that drinking a beer would help make the hangover go away. I didn’t share on my self-reflection the previous night, so they all made me share after we were done with dinner. It was a great sharing session and we all cuddled in support :). Connor decided to Uber home

Connor decided to Uber home. I stayed with Lucas and Sahar a bit, even though it made me late to work. We talked about enneagram personalities and they helped me better understand that I was a 3. Sahar and I had a bit of a bonding moment over it since, at that time, she also thought she was a 3. We watched a video on 3s that perfectly explained how I work, function, and think as a person [Picture #19]. I remember watching the YouTube video and breaking out into nervous laughter because of how true the description was. I will always love Lucas and Sahar for pushing me personally and helping me discover my personality.
