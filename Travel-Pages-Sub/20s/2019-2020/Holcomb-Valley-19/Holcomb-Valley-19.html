Holcomb Valley 2019

  • With Mom & Doug
  • July 2019
  • TR and lead climbing with UCI

While I was home during the summer of 2019, I accompanied the UCI adventure squad to Holcomb valley to climb! Doug was leading the group, so mom and I decided to join in. It was honestly sooooo much fun and was the first time I led outdoors! My best onsite was this really easy 10a with lots of rest. I did attempt a 10c but it was crazy hard. I guess I now know my limits lol.

This trip was amazing! I am so glad that mom suggested we accompany the group Doug was leading. We got up way early in the morning with Doug to pick up the students at the ARC. After picking everyone up, we began our drive to Big Bear / Holcomb Valley. I was asleep for most of the drive but when I woke up, I was surrounded by the beauty of Big Bear [Picture #1]. There was a huge lake, many trees, and great vibes.

We parked at a designated parking lot and used the restroom before heading to the climbing area. There was a team of runners from nearby all finishing their run around then. Looked like a cross country team. Some of the guys were actually totally my type ;). We regrouped, got back in our cars, and begun the 15min drive to the base of the climbing area.

After driving on some bumpy roads, we arrived. There were actually a good number of other cars so we suspected it would be a busy day on the rock. We started on our 15min approach hike [Picture #2]. We ended up walking by a ton of other climbers. Eventually we stopped at our first rock wall which was a tall but relatively easy looking 5.9. This ended up being the first climb I half led! This is because the person before me could only make it up halfway and then gave up. I decided to finish the climb and was excited to set up the anchor. All the climbing pictures of the day. We did a few other random climbs before I got to my first two full lead climbs! There was a 5.6 slab and a pretty vertical 5.10a. The 5.6 was pretty easy but the 5.10a was a bit freaky to start. The first move was off of two underclings into another undercling on some sketchy ground. I was pretty scared at first but once I committed to the first move, it was smooth sailing and so much fun! It was so nice to finally get this experience under my belt. I also got cocky and attempted a 5.10c that I could only get halfway up. Doug had to climb to where I got and downclimb to save the gear. Oooops lol.

Eventually it was time to go. On the way back to the vans, I took a very nice photo of some flowers during golden hour that I used as my background photo for a long time [Picture #13]. I was so proud of it :). Also got a nice photo of the sunset [Picture #14]. What a great day!
