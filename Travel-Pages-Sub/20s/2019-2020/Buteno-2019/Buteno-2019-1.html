Buteno 2019

  • With Connor, Matt, Dixon, Reece, Daniel, & Jack
  • Oct 18th-20th 2019
  • Remote Boys Trip

This trip was the second week of training at Accenture’s NA training headquarters. We were able to interact with new analyst from all over NA and learn more about how Accenture functions as a company. It was an amazing opportunity to get closer with my start class, grow my understanding of the consulting world, and perform at my best. I will cherish the memories and friendships I gained from this training. In my opinion, Accenture’s orientation + new joiner training is very effective. This training also gave me an opportunity to visit Nate in Chicago. It was so nice to catch up with him and see how his life is going in this new city.

Day 1

Picture #3

This was the start of my second week at Accenture! The first week was great, my start class is amazing, social, and everyone vibes well with each other. However, it was so exciting being able to travel for the first time as a consultant! Our second week of training was located at St. Charles in Chicago, which is Accenture’s very own corporate training center. I decided I would make the most sense to leave for St. Charles on Sunday so I could still get the entire weekend in the bay area.

Left for St. Charles in the morning. Everything was paid for which was quite nice :). Got on my direct flight to Chicago and was off! OMG when I arrived, I was picked up in a freaking full stretch limo [Pictures #1 & #2]. So fancy :). I shared the limo with a few other analysts from the Atlanta office. The drive was around 30mins. I thought about taking a nap in the limo but that just didn’t make that much sense.

Picture #4
Picture #5

We eventually arrived at the beautiful campus of St. Charles [Picture #3]. It was basically built on a golf course out in the middle of a suburban area. When I arrived, I ran into Bella, Joy, and Harrison. I was on the same floor as Bella so we went to our rooms together. Not going to lie, the rooms in this place were tiny! Like the size of a cruise ship room. Honestly, it did the job. I went to go eat dinner on my own since I felt like having a semi relaxing night.

Dinner was surprisingly great! There were so many amazing options and I was STARVING :). I was FTing mom and just catching but saw that John, one of our Accenture trainers was coming my way, I quickly said bye to mom and joined John and another one of his friends for dinner. Turns out John friend was one of the planners for this entire training. Very impressive. I finished my dinner and eventually took my leave. I hungout in my room for a bit before forcing myself to go lift at the gym [Picture #4]. No one was really there so I had the place to myself. I just remember being too full to go all out on lifting haha.

On my way back from the gym, I passed a topgolf trip flyer that caught my eye [Picture #5]!. I decided to convince a good number of my start class to come! I went back up to my room, FTed mom again and then went to sleep.

Day 2

Picture #6
Picture #7

I got up early and got ready for my first day of St. Charles training [Picture #6]! I met up with Bella and we made our way to the main ballroom area. We grabbed a few pastries and coffee and found a space for our class to sit and hangout. The first part of the training was all pretty basic stuff[Picture #7]. We went over the week’s agenda, Accenture principals, and heard from a panel of analysts. We eventually broke up into groups and all of us headed out on our own. My first group was with John, our very own NorCal training teacher, and another manager named Jackie. My training group included Shamsya, Aileen, Nivi, and Briar. Not gonna lie, our team was an absolute powerhouse :). Also, our teachers, especially Jackie, were amazing! So informative and had a high set of standards that I resonated with.

The training at St. Charles included our premade teams creating an example client by selecting things such as industry, product, and background. Throughout the week, we would be given a number of prompts and had to present on a solution or task. It was basically a mock case competition lol. What I noticed was that some teams took this activity more seriously than others. My team was full of try hards so we dominated :).

Picture #8
Picture #9

After a long and tiring first day, our entire start class from NorCal decided we needed a drink. We basically took over the mini bar at the Q Center lol [Picture #8]. This set a precedent for the rest of the week. Word started to spread that the Q pub was going to be open that night. Apparently, it was just some full-on bar that people on the training grounds could party at. Our entire group decided we would attend after dinner. Some of us went back to our rooms to freshen up and then our entire class went down to the Cafeteria to eat.

We all hungout for a bit before heading to the Q pub. It was right on the campus and actually was a pretty large space! There was a full 360-degree bar, a karaoke area, and tons of people. We had learned during our NorCal training that Justin loves T-Swift so we made him karaoke it in front of everyone else at the bar [Picture #9]! Since the song was Wildest Dreams, I obvi had to join in! What a song full of interesting memories. After leaving the bar, we all went back to our rooms and got ready for the next day of training.

During one of the breaks, I drew SpongeBob SquarePants under the prompt “Favorite Celebrities” lol [Picture #12].

A couple things happened today. During our NorCal training, one of my fellow analysts came out to me and another analyst that they were LGBTQ+ and I accidentally outed them when with another group of analysts in our start class. I was disappointed in myself and apologized. It brought up an interesting conversation amongst us, but my action was still inappropriate. Tonight, was the networking banquet esq night [Picture #17]. Our entire group kept on joking about how clickily of a start class we were. At some point I went up to Jackie to talk to her about my experience so far and how much I have liked it. I thought it was important to do since she was a very capable leader and seemed to have her life figured out haha. She said that I was her favorite from the entire training referencing my executive presence as a determining factor. Overall it was a confidence boost of sorts. This party slowly transitioned into all of us going to hanging out at the mini bar area. Jackie, my instructor, kinda came out of nowhere and saved me from the situation. It was around 1am when I eventually went back to my room.
