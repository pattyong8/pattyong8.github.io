Irvine Summer 2019

  • With Family
  • Summer 2019
  • Home for the summer after graduation

After backpacking around Southeast Asia, I came home for around 2 weeks before starting my full-time job in San Francisco. I caught up with a number of friends, had some quality family time, and ate amazing food. (Taiko twice!) It was a relaxing period before I had to enter into the real world. School had passed me at this point and instead, directly in front of me, was post-graduate life. I cherished these two weeks and made the most of them!

After coming home, I would eat lunch with mom practical every day during her lunch break. We got food items such as Chipotle, Blaze Pizza, and even food from the Mitsuwa food court [Pictures #1, #2, #4, & #5]. I also made sure to climb at the UCI gym while I was here [Picture #3].

This break gave me the perfect window to catch up with a few friends. These included Tatum, Jake, Kyle, and Scotty.

Tatum and I did a ton of stuff within these two weeks. We went to Barley Forge Brewing Company, Pressed Yogurt, Taiko and El Ranchito [Pictures #9, #10, & #11]. When we went to Taiko, I kid you not, it was the longest pre-open line I have ever seen (at least 30 people)! We got all my favorite sushi dishes [Pictures #12, #13, #14, & #15]. Tatum said Taiko was in fact amazing and she would definitely be bringing her dad in the future.

I also got to get Bear Flag with Jake, climbing for a bit with Kyle, and hit up the anti-mall near John Wayne with Scotty [Pictures #16, #17, & #18]. Oh and don’t forget about all the great food [Pictures #19 & #20]! :)

Since this was the last long break I was expecting to have once I enter into the full-time working world, I made the most of the time I had with my family. It was nice having Doug around since I know it will make my mom’s life better once Vincent and I transition into post-grad life. He is really great and treats her well. I am happy they are together [Picture #21]! :)

Went to Taiko with the family, it is a tradition and a must since mom loves it! Got all the best dishes. Since Aiko san was our sushi bar chef, we got extra fish on all plates [Pictures #22, #23, #24, & #25].

For my graduation celebrate, (and to be honest, for Vincent’s birthday, Christmas, Thanksgiving, all holidays, significant days, etc. lol) we went to Mastro’s Ocean Club for dinner [Pictures #27, #28, #29, #30, & #31]. We don’t go out to nice place like ever, so we made this dinner a celebrate for everything! Doug and Mom got fish while Vincent and I got steaks. TBH, the steaks were only okay. Maybe it’s because I am not a steak connoisseur yet lol. Mom made sure to get the buttercake at the end and OMG let me tell you, this butter cake was literally the best restaurant dessert I have had in a while! Apparently, this is one of the things that Mastro’s is famous for.

Lastly, we made it out for one of the UCI group paddleboard/kayak sessions [Pictures #32, #33, #34, #35, & #36]. Vincent and I stuck with mom during the entire time since we were not moving that fast. I was in the same kayak as mom. We got some great photos before sunset and even at night! Snapchat has some amazing filter that makes it look like the nighttime sky was full of stars [Pictures #37, & #38]! After this, Mom, Vincent, and I hot tubed and chatted. It was a nice way to wrap up the two weeks I had at home [Picture #39]! :)
