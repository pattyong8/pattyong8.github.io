4th of July in Coronado

  • With Friends
  • Jul 3rd-6th 2022
  • San Diego, CA

Our first annual 4th of July trip to Coronado was unforgettable, filled with beach time, beer die, and perfect weather. All our friends from San Francisco gathered at Olivia's house for a three-day holiday bash. Drinks flowed, memories were made, and new friendships were formed. We spent our days soaking up the sun on the beach and our evenings enjoying lively conversations and laughter. The highlight was watching the spectacular fireworks display over the water, a perfect end to an amazing trip.

Pool & Beer Die

When I first arrived at Olivia's, I went directly into the pool and spent about an hour tanning on a pool floatie with Hannah. Hannah and I caught up on life and enjoyed the sun. Eventually, it was my turn to play beer die. This was one of the first times I truly played a ton of beer die since college. To be frank, I was terrible, but it was tons of fun. My other friends were on another level with their hand-eye coordination. It was nice being able to hop in and out of the pool to cool down between catching rays. Bailey, Olivia's family dog, was running around and chasing a ball toy that we were all throwing. Bailey even got in the pool!

Nightime & Fireworks

After the sun went down, our group continued our debauchery. Thankfully, Olivia had thoughtfully coordinated catering for our group, a detail that could have easily been overlooked. Without it, we all would have been much more drunk and hungry. I became good friends with Olivia's brothers, Jared and Jack, and spent time hanging out with them and their friends. On the night of July 4th, we went up to Olivia's roof and watched all the fireworks set off around San Diego. Apparently, this was a coordinated effort by SeaWorld. It was truly a beautiful sight.

Beach Days

The beach days were INSANE! We literally dug out a 4-5 foot moat to play beer pong. At some point, the patrolling lifeguards said we needed to fill the moat up before we finished since it could be a hazard. Footballs were thrown, spikeball was played, and waves were bodysurfed. Our second beach day was at Coronado Beach, where we lounged with all the other beachgoers and spent quality time together. I caught up a ton with Ryan and Cohen. What a great way to spend the 4th of July and a tradition I will definitely look forward to continuing.
