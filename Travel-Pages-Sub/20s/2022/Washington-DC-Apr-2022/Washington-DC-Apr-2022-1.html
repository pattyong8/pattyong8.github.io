Washington DC 2022

  • With Family
  • Apr 2022
  • National monuments, KGJ celebration, White House Tour

Our entire family decided to go visit Vincent and Erin in Washington DC. From exploring the National Mall to the White House spring gardens tour, we saw all the wonderful sights of our nations capital. This was one of the more recent family trips post Covid and was an opporunity to get out of the pandemic routine. During my time in Washington DC, I was also able to meet up with a good number of my high school friends and eat at a ton of Vincent and Erin's favroite restaurants.

Day 1

Since I landed later in the day, the first day was a bit of a wash. I arrived at Vinny's apartment and put down my stuff before getting ready for dinner. Shortly after arriving, Mom, Doug, and Coleman met us at the house, and we headed over to the restaurant. Vinny made reservations at Founding Farmers Fishers & Bakers, a wonderful restaurant with a variety of dishes. One of the more notable things about the restaurant was the water fixture by the bathrooms. It was a bathtub water fountain! We got a few dishes to share, including the ahi tuna salad and guacamole.

After dinner, we decided to head home and stopped by the Eastdil Secured office on the way. I showed the exterior of the building to everyone before we headed to a nearby crepe shop. We all got crepes for dessert and decided to call it a night shortly after.

Day 2

Day 2 was packed with adventure and fun. We started by exploring a nearby sculpture park and a few museum exhibits before visiting the Department of Justice. We happened to visit the day after the Honorable Ketanji Brown Jackson was sworn into the Supreme Court. It was truly a momentous occasion, as she is the first Black woman to serve on the Supreme Court. There were celebrations outside the Supreme Court, and we got to join in the festivities. Various organizations created posters, organized events, and played music. The excitement in the air was palpable.

After exploring the Supreme Court, we headed over to the National Mall. We managed to get tickets for the Natural History Museum and spent a good amount of time exploring the different exhibits. It was an amazing collection of artifacts and knowledge. While at the dinosaur exhibit, we were asked to take a survey, which we happily participated in.

After exploring the various sights that DC had to offer, we parted ways with Doug and Coleman for some quality family time. We started off at a bar called Chicken + Whiskey, which had a speakeasy in the back! It had such a cool vibe, and I was glad we all grabbed a drink together. I think this was one of the first times after college that our family had drinks together. Making memories :). Vincent got a beer, Mom got a fruity cocktail, and I got one of their signature cocktails that was delivered in a glass bottle. It was very interesting and the first time I had seen that. The cocktails were amazing and really hit the spot. Mom got a bit drunk off her one drink, which was characteristic of her tolerance level.

After drinks, we continued to the main event: a one-Michelin-star sushi dinner at Sushi Taro. When we arrived at the restaurant, I noticed it was not as packed as I expected for a Michelin-starred establishment. Also, it wasn't omakase; it was Ã  la carte. We went ahead and placed our orders, then waited for the food to arrive. This was the first time we were able to have a nice dinner together, and it did not disappoint. We got all our favorites and even some dessert. I even got to celebrate my birthday with my family!

After dinner, we headed to a nearby arcade bar where Vinny and I competed against each other on the basketball game. Mom even participated, which was a blast! Vinny won most of the games we played. It was a fantastic way to end the night.

Day 3

Day 3 was full of activities as well. We started the morning with the very rare and perfectly timed White House flower gardens tour. This tour only happens once a year, and the tickets are hard to get. We had to arrive very early to secure our spot in line. It was well worth it since we were able to get within feet of the White House building. The staff had set up pictures around the lawn of prior presidents walking the grounds. There was also a live band that played for all the visitors.

After exploring the White House grounds, we made our way to lunch. Vinny picked out a place called Hank's Oysters that had a ton of fresh seafood. We got oysters for the table, ahi tuna poke, and a seafood plate to share. For my entree, I ordered seared scallops with a carb-based side. To be honest, it was like a 5/10, and my dish had a mini crab in it. The seafood lacked flavor, and the dishes were a bit underdone for the price.

After lunch, it was time for Mom, Doug, and Coleman to fly back to Southern California. They had already been there for a full week before I arrived, so they left before me. Mom, Vinny, and I shared a heartfelt embrace before saying goodbye until next time. The memories we made will not be forgotten and will be cherished deeply.

After Mom, Doug, and Coleman left for the airport, Vinny, Erin, and I met up with some of our high school friends. It was great catching up with Matilda, Tatum, Alex, and a few others. We started the evening with drinks at a nearby bar that Tatum recommended, then a few of us split off for dinner. We chose a restaurant called Ambar, and it was phenomenal. The tapas-style dishes featured high-quality ingredients, and it was evident why the place was Michelin recommended. As the trip came to an end, I reflected on the great friends I was able to catch up with, quality family time, and all the amazing food we ate.
