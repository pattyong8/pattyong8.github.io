Santa Barbara 2022

  • With Davis & Friends
  • Apr 2022
  • Santa Barbara, CA

This trip was a celebration of Davis' upcoming graduation. With just a few months left until the end of his college journey, I couldn't miss the chance to visit and experience the UCSB lifestyle. From indulging in numerous delicious meals to enjoying picture-perfect beach days, we made the most of Davis' final days as a college senior. Highlights of the trip included playing beer die with the swim team guys and savoring Omakase at Sushi by Scratch. I was thrilled to be there for Davis, helping him take graduation photos and soaking up the sunshine together.

After landing at the airport, I quickly made my way out of the terminal to meet Davis. Thankfully, UCSB is close to the Santa Barbara airport, making the ride very convenient. Our first stop was East Beach, a local poke taco shop located in the parking lot of a batting cages facility. Davis shared a pro tip: ask for the spicy mayo sauce on the side to drizzle over the tacos. He was absolutely rightâ€”it added a delicious layer of richness to the already fantastic poke tacos. The quality of these tacos was evident in every bite.

Once we finished our poke tacos, we headed back to Davis' place at UCSB. Even though I'd been there a few times before, the stunning view and proximity to the water never ceased to amaze me. It's incredible that they lived literally on the cliffs of Santa Barbara. Davis was still living with Weston, and they had a great setup. We hung out at the house for a bit before heading to campus to take some graduation pictures for Davis. We chose golden hour for its prime lighting, making the photos even more special. We visited the typical spots at UCSB to capture his graduation memories and made a lovely late afternoon out of it. Of course, we made sure to send the best shots to his mom.

We retired back to Davis' place after the photo session. The sun was setting, casting a beautiful glow over the cliffs and the ocean. We spent the evening reminiscing about his college journey and discussing future plans. It was a perfect way to wind down after a busy day.

The next day was packed with drinking, partying, and endless games of beer die. The scene reminded me of how my friends and I celebrated our final semester of senior year. Our drink of choice was Topo Chico, perfect for a sunny day. Davis and I teamed up for numerous rounds of beer die, switching off with other teams. We spent the day shirtless, soaking in the rays and enjoying the carefree atmosphere.

At some point, the group decided to create their own "Floattopia," a beloved UCSB tradition where students float in the ocean on various devices, drink, and blast music. Though no longer endorsed by the university due to environmental concerns, we made sure to avoid dumping our drink cans into the ocean. Our flotation device of choice was an air mattress, which comfortably fit four other guys. We floated around for a few hours with the entire swim house, enjoying the sun and camaraderie. It was such a fun experience, one I would have missed out on if I hadn't visited.

After floating around all afternoon, Davis and I took some time to rest before dinner. I hadn't told Davis what the evening had in store, only that we were going to see one of my friends who lived in Santa Barbara. In reality, I had planned a special omakase dinner surprise to celebrate his graduation. The plan was to dine at Sushi by Scratch, a one Michelin star sushi restaurant in Santa Barbara. When dinner time arrived, I signaled that it was time to head out. I guided Davis, saying we were heading to old town Santa Barbara. We found a nice area and parked in an open space. As we walked up to the restaurant, Davis started to suspect something special was planned. When I finally revealed our evening's plans, he was blown away!

Sushi by Scratch was an experience that combined food with theater. With only a few rounds of seating each night, guests were asked to arrive early. Upon arrival, Davis and I were greeted by the host and given welcome drinks as we waited for the other dinner guests. Davis, experiencing his first proper omakase, was both excited and nervous. I was thrilled to surprise him with such a special treat for his graduation. Once the other guests arrived, we were ushered into the private sushi bar, ready to begin our culinary adventure.

The chefs kicked off the dinner by announcing the full menu, which was displayed behind them, and gave an introduction about their team and individual backgrounds. They then began preparing our 15-course omakase. Davis and I were blown away. Each piece of sushi was perfectly paired with thoughtfully selected ingredients, and the quality of the fish was exceptional. Our favorites included the O Toro, yellowtail, and scallop. Even the uni was outstanding, with a sweet and delicate flavor. It was a truly memorable dining experience.

The next day marked my last day in Santa Barbara. Before departing, Davis and I decided to savor a few more meals and spend time at one of Santa Barbara's beautiful beaches. Our day began with a meal at Boathouse, an idyllic restaurant nestled in one of the beach inlets along the coast. We ended up waiting for an hour, so we took the opportunity to sit at the beach and enjoy the perfect Santa Barbara weather. As we lounged on the beach, we caught up on our current lives and shared thoughts on our aspirations for the future.

In the afternoon, we decided to try a random Chinese restaurant we found through Google search. Meet Up turned out to be a relatively new Sichuan restaurant about 15 minutes from UCSB. To our surprise, both Davis and I were thoroughly impressed with the food. One standout dish was their fried fish, which genuinely dazzled my taste buds with its exceptional flavors. Later on, Davis was so impressed that he brought a few of his friends back to Meet Up to share the experience. Overall, this trip was a fantastic way to spend time with one of my closest friends, celebrating his graduation from one of the best universities in the US. It was a memorable journey filled with great food, beach days, and cherished moments together.
