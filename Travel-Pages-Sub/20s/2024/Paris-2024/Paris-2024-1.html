Paris Olympics 2024

  • With Parth, Quinn, & Jake
  • Jul 20th-Aug 1st 2024
  • Paris, France

My trip to Paris for the 2024 Olympics with my best friends is one I will never forget. From watching thrilling Olympic events to dining at renowned brasseries, we experienced the full magic of Paris from a unique perspective. We indulged in delicious duck dishes and visited the Chase Reserve lounge perhaps a few too many times. It was the perfect international adventure before starting my new job.

Day 1

After spending two weeks in Southern California, I headed back to NYC to pack for my trip to Paris for the 2024 Olympics. I was incredibly excited to explore one of the most famous cities in the world with Quinn, Parth, and Jake. My journey began around 5 PM from Newark Airport on a direct Air France flight. This was the nicest international plane I had ever been on, with comfortable seats, massive TV screens, and even Bluetooth audio, which was a first for me. Instead of sleeping, I watched several movies during the short 7-hour flight.

Throughout the flight, I found myself interacting regularly with a very charming flight attendant. Our conversations were casual; he would ask if I enjoyed my meal and offer me a drink when I got up from the bathroom. Towards the end of the flight, I asked him for a few food and drink recommendations. His heavy French accent made it hard to understand him, so I had him write down his suggestions, planning to look them up later. To my surprise, one of his recommendations was the gay district in Paris! In hindsight, it might have been fun to ask him out while in Paris, but alas.

Once I cleared immigration, I headed for the city center of Paris. We all decided to use a temporary 1-7 day unlimited metro pass from the local transportation service. Since I arrived at the Airbnb four hours early, I waited for check-in at a nearby park, enjoying the sight of the city waking up. The park grew more populated as the hours passed, and the weather, in the low 70s, made for a pleasant reading environment.

Finally, it was time to check in, and I headed to the Airbnb to meet the owner. He handed me the keys and took off for the South of France. Being the first to arrive, I decided to go for a quick run to explore the surrounding area. This turned out to be a mistake as I was sleep-deprived and not a regular runner. I used up most of my adrenaline reserves on this effort and should have saved it for the day's activities. On the bright side, I did get to run along the Seine for a bit.

Jake texted me on my way back, letting me know he had arrived. I rushed home to prevent him from waiting and took a quick post-run shower. About 45 minutes later, Parth and Quinn arrived. We were all ecstatic to be in Paris together and couldn't wait to explore the neighborhood. We were hungry and started by grabbing lunch at TournBride, a local French café. The menu prices were high, as expected in Paris, though I soon realized that Paris was cheaper than New York and San Francisco. Opting for the duck leg confit with potatoes, I found this to be a staple at most French restaurants. Naturally, we had wine with our meal.

Picture #19

After lunch, we strolled around the Latin Quarter and made our way to the Panthéon, admiring Paris's beautiful architecture along the way. Every block seemed to feature a monument or notable landmark, a stark contrast to the United States. In need of another drink, we discovered a charming alley filled with cafés and restaurants. Here, we enjoyed beers and coffees while catching up on Quinn and Parth's travels across Europe, giving Jake and me a chance to get to know each other better. A memorable moment occurred when a friendly local overheard our American accents and struck up a conversation, revealing she was also from Southern California. It was interesting to realize how Americans tend to stand out wherever we go.

We walked past Hôtel de Ville, one of the Olympic celebration venues, and spent about 20 minutes taking photos, milling around, and enjoying the decorations. Next, we headed to the Arc de Triomphe, passing by the Louvre, where we saw a group of locals practicing their dance moves. Tired from a long day of travel and walking, our focus shifted from exploration to food and getting back to the Airbnb. Although our dinner was underwhelming, it was a new dish I had never heard of and was great to try. Parth and I took the bunk room, while Quinn and Jake took the main and trundle beds.
