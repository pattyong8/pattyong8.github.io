Catalina 2024

  • With Matt, Connor, and Jack
  • July 12th - 14th 2024
  • Catalina, CA

The first boys' Catalina trip was unforgettable. Matt, whose family had owned a house on the island for generations, graciously hosted us. We kicked back with cold beers, dove into the island's nightlife, and spent lazy afternoons soaking up the sun on the beach. It was the perfect mix of relaxation and adventure, a much-needed recharge for everyone.

Day 1: Island Tour and Quality Time

We kicked off our Catalina weekend at the Dana Point port, where Matt, Connor, Jack, and I met to start our journey. Matt had invited us for a much-needed getaway, promising beach time, pickleball, and a chance to recharge. I arrived just in time to catch Matt’s mom, who came to see us off—a sweet gesture that set the tone for the trip.

As luck would have it, we discovered that Paulia and her friends were also heading to Catalina the same weekend. Talk about a small world! We made plans to catch up while boarding the ferry, excitement buzzing in the air.

The ferry ride over was packed, a clear sign of Catalina's popularity as a summer destination. I dozed off for part of the ride, needing a nap after the hectic morning. When I woke up, I marveled at the number of people heading to the island. It brought back memories of my only other trip to Catalina—summer camp in grade school. This time, I was eager to make new memories with some of my closest friends.

After arriving, we headed straight to Matt’s family’s house, an idyllic island retreat that perfectly captured the laid-back Catalina vibe. Once we dropped off our bags, it was time to explore. Connor hadn’t eaten yet, so we made our way to The Lobster Trap, one of the island’s best spots. While Connor fueled up, the rest of us enjoyed pints of Great White, a fantastic wheat beer at an impressively low price. Jack and I tried to convince the guys to order Stardust—a move that would’ve set quite the tone for the day!

After lunch, we wandered around Avalon, soaking in the lively atmosphere. That’s when we ran into Marissa Fink—a blast from the past! She was visiting the island with her mom, and we spent about 20 minutes catching up before heading back to the house for our next adventure: a self-led golf cart tour of the island.

Matt took the wheel, guiding us through Avalon and sharing childhood stories, iconic landmarks, and the best lookout point. The view was breathtaking: Avalon sprawled out below, dotted with boats, and anchored by the Casino, a Marine Conservation Area. It was a moment to soak in the beauty of the island and appreciate the trip’s serendipity.

On our way back, Marissa texted, inviting us to meet at the Marlin Club. We got ready and headed over to join her and her mom. The evening was a mix of nostalgia and hilarity—reminiscing about our school days and snapping photos with a group of older ladies who struck up a conversation with Jack and Matt.

After drinks, we returned to Matt’s house and gathered around the fireplace. It was the perfect opportunity to catch up on each other’s lives and share updates in a setting that felt worlds away from our usual routines. Moments like these remind me why I cherish these friendships so much.

Day 2: Pickleball, Beach, and Night Out

Little did I know, Matt had gotten up early the next morning to secure prime beach spots, setting up chairs and towels in the perfect location. This would be our home base for the day after our pickleball session. Once we were up and ready, we hopped in the golf cart and headed to the pickleball courts. On the way, we passed the island’s quirky 9-hole golf course—a charming little spot more suited for casual fun than serious competition.

At the pickleball courts, Matt’s connections came in clutch. He knew a few of the regular players, and they kindly let us in. We spent a few hours playing, rotating partners and teams. I was definitely the weakest link, but it was still a blast! Matt mentioned that his parents were avid pickleball players, always giving him tips, which explained why he was a natural.

After our pickleball session, we headed back to the house to freshen up before hitting the beach. With Matt’s well-chosen setup waiting for us, we settled in for an afternoon of cards, sunshine, and relaxation. We dove into a marathon game of Hearts, playing seven or eight rounds. By the end, it was a close race between Matt and me for the lead, Jack nearly shot the moon, and Connor was dominating. Eventually, we set the cards aside and took some time to ourselves.

While Matt read his book and Connor and Jack explored town, I decided to go snorkeling near the Casino. The underwater world there was incredible. Unlike any other spot I’ve dived, the fish here were completely unbothered by my presence—they even seemed to make eye contact! I probably startled a few scuba divers by free diving to their depths without any gear, but it was exhilarating.

After snorkeling, I met up with the guys as they were wrapping up at the beach, and we headed back to the house to prep for our next adventure: mini golf. Matt was especially excited, sharing that his family made it a tradition every time they visited. It turned into a full-on competition. Connor and I started off behind, but I managed an epic shot on a maze-like hole that usually takes eight strokes. I nailed it in three by chipping over a wall—let’s go! Despite that, Jack and Matt were unstoppable, and Connor, though unlucky, was a great sport.

Back at the house, we shifted gears to dinner. A quick trip to the nearby Vons provided the ingredients for a rice dish made with Rice-A-Roni, whipped up efficiently in the house’s well-equipped kitchen. As we ate, the pregame began. Matt’s mom had thoughtfully stocked the fridge with beer, so we indulged while playing “F the Dealer.” Jack set the vibe with a house light rave and an EDM playlist—classic Jack Connolly energy.

Later, we joined the girls at the Marlin Club for one last night out. While catching up, I met up with someone I’d been talking to, and we headed to the Casino to hang out, taking in the ocean breeze and the island’s nighttime magic. Meanwhile, Jack befriended an older gentleman at the bar, spending a full hour in deep conversation about life—another quintessential Jack Connolly moment.

The next morning, it was time to say goodbye. Reflecting on the trip, I couldn’t help but feel grateful for the time spent with my best friends, the laughs, the memories, and the start of a new annual tradition. Cheers to many more Catalina adventures to come!
