Butano 2017

  • With Room 5 boys, Dixon, Shane, Ungar, and Nate
  • Mar 17th-19th, 2017
  • College Boys' Trip

One day while we were at UC Berkeley. Connor brought up the fact that his family would always go to a cabin in the woods and spend some quality unplugged family time. After one of our friends asked if we could all go, the Butano tradition was born. This is where some of my lifelong college buddies and I created really close friendships. One of my favorite memories was when we all hiked up a stream to explore the area and go cliff jumping! Can't wait to see what other memories we can create on future Butano trips.

Hiking Upstream

While in Butano, we made it a point to explore the natural beauty around us. On our second day there, we started our day with a hike through the dense forest until we came upon the spot Connor was looking for. He led us down to a clearing by the stream where we started our upstream hike. I will never forget the curveball we threw into our nature hike lol. After walking for about 10 mins, we found a natural water hole that acted as a natural ice bath. Each of us took a refreshing plunge.

Cliff Jumping

On our way back, we stopped at the cliff jumping area and jumped off a few times. Boy it was scary but also exhilarating. There was a bank on the other side of the jump zone that we all swam to after jumping. We were able to warm up in the sunlight and take a few photos. There was also a natural water slide at the bottom of the mini waterfall. Someone in the area put up a rope-like ladder so that people could pull themselves up to the top. I remember Shane and I fell on our butts and went down the slide together.

Cabin Shenanigans

After a full day of activites, most people took a quite nap. I remember that Matt refused to sleep and instead, took a bath, drank a glass of wine, and listened to country music. We made a wondeful pasta house dinner that night. Once we were all fed, we started to drink a bit. One of the funniest moments was when Jack slapped Matt super hard in the face on a dare. What a time and 1/2.
